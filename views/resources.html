ACE YOUR INTERVIEW

It's essential to be able to show the hiring manager that you're the best person for the job and have the skills and credentials they need for a successful hire.

TIPS FOR OFFLINE INTERVIEW

  • Arrive Early

    Arrive for your interview about 5-10 minutes early. Good employers value punctuality, and if you arrive even a minute late, you will likely give a poor first impression.

  • Honesty Is What We Treasure

    Be Honest with the Inetrviewer
    Answer with the facts as best you can. Be open and confident, providing valid reasons for any part of your employment history that you are not proud of.

  • Follow-Up After the Interview

    It's a good idea to follow-up on an interview within 24 hours. Email our interviewer ; or, if you want to make a lasting impression, send an email thank you. In the email or letter, thank the interviewer for his or her time



TIPS FOR ONLINE INTERVIEW

  • Test All Equipment Prior to Interview Time

    Do a run-through at least an hour before your interview time. And then do another run-through around 30 minutes before the interview time.

  • Dress Professionally

    Nothing too flashy or suggestive with clothing, we would recommend a plain white shirt and black trousers.

  • Turn Off Before the Interview Starts

    Remember to turn off all apps and programs that might interrupt the interview. Also, silence your phone.



TIPS FOR PHONE CALL INTERVIEW

  • Prepare

    Research about the role like you would for a face-to-face interview but over the phone it’s harder to build rapport with someone, so you really need to impress with what you say and know.

  • Enunciate

    it’s important your responses are spoken clearly and kept concise to make yourself heard. Be sure what to speak and remain calm.

  • Be confident

    To make a good first impression on call, remember to be personable and sell yourself through your knowledge and expertise.
